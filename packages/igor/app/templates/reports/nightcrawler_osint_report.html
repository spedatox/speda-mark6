01Assessment

Bottom line

Seven domains registered between March and June share a hosting fingerprint and a reused favicon hash — one operator, high confidence. Tying that operator to the entity trading publicly as Vellum Group rests on two overlapping registrant artefacts and holds only at moderate. Nothing malicious was observed live on any host; the cluster reads as staged and not yet fired.

02Findings

F—01

Certificate issuance came in one batch

High

All seven domains presented certificates from the same authority inside a 41-minute window on 12 March, with identical SAN ordering. Transparency logs show no other domains in that batch — the window is the strongest single link in the cluster.

F—02

One cached registrant record reaches the brand

Moderate

A WHOIS snapshot taken before privacy protection was applied exposes an administrative address on a corporate domain that appears in the entity's public filings. The artefact is single-sourced and its cache timestamp cannot be independently verified. That ceiling is what caps KJ-2, and no amount of restating it raises the confidence.

F—03

No payload was observed on any host

High

Every passive observation across the window returned a parked response or a default server page. This is not evidence of benign intent — it is exactly what pre-operational staging looks like from the outside.
